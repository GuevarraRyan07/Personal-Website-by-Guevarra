<p style="background-color: gold;">
<b>To be honest, I was born to spare my whole childhood within a small group and within my family as possible. I am always hanging with kids whom my parents only trust, and these kids will only be either my siblings or mu cousins. I do not have even got a friend from school, maybe just some acquaintances with the kids around our neighborhood, but my Mom does not allow me to hang out with them for a long period of time. I understand that they are just trying to protect me as a kid and it has never diminish my spirit to enjoy my chidhood stage. Me and my cousins used to have play dates and it is such a wonderful experience, if you ask me. Maybe it sounds being marginalized for others, but for me, as a kid that time, as long as I am comfortable and having fun with them, it does not matter how small the group I am included in. And I will forever treasure and cherish the moments I spare my childhood with them. They would always be my allies no matter what.  
</b><br><br> 
<b>As I was growing up, I could rely on my friends to help me with my homework, to aid me with problems at home, or to simply be there and have fun. We started building an unbreakable friendship because my pals and I had a lot in common, enjoyed playing the same games, and enjoyed enjoying life. Many of my friends stayed at my house while I was growing up when they had issues at their homes, which fostered trust between us. The relationships we create with our friends while we are young are vital to our growth because, as the saying goes, that's when we're truly looking for attachment. These friendships also tend to be the ones that endure the longest. My childhood buddies helped shape who I am now; their sense of humor, sense of adventure, and trust forged a relationship that has lasted to this day.
</b></p>
